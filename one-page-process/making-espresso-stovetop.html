Making Espresso Stovetop Style

An easy and cost effective way to make espresso on the stove without the need of an expensive machine.

What you'll need:

  • a small, two-chambered moka pot
  • espresso ground coffee

Instructions:

  1. Separate the three parts of the moka pot: bottom chamber, metal portafilter, and spouted top chamber.
  2. Fill the bottom chamber of your moka pot with water that's fresh off the boil.
  3. Completely fill the metal portafilter with ground coffee, distribute evenly and don't pack it down. Place portafilter inside the bottom chamber.
  4. Screw the bottom and spouted top with the filter secured inside.
  5. Place the moka pot on the stove and keep the heat low.
  6. When the water in the bottom chamber approaches a boil, the pressure will push a stream of coffee slowly and methodically through the upper chamber. When you hear a hissing, bubbling sound, remove the pot from the heat.
  7. Pour the freshly brewed espresso in a cup and enjoy.
