    Small Vastness: Life in Cabo Polonio

    8 May 2016

    • Words: Espacio Living
    • Photos: Javier Csecs
    The silhouette of this minimal ranch, almost a mirage among the dunes of the Uruguayan coast. Style and simplicity within a few meters.

    Cabo Polonio enjoys a reputation of being a mythic land, nourished by the unusual anecdotes from both the people who live there all year and those who choose it as a destination for the summer. It is precisely these people who choose Cabo despite its isolation and lack of amenities such as electricity that give it something so special. Gabriela Saludes, the owner of this ranch, gives evidence to this. After years of spending her summers in the ares, she wanted to have her own place there. She liked this little house and tried to buy it from its original owners, two Uruguayans located in the Canary Islands. From there, she tracked them down to make them an offer to purchase and their tenacity was rewarded by the owners giving them the ranch. The only condition was to continue employing the girl who took care of it.

    With several improvements made by a team of local builders, José Luis San Martín Sena and Gustavo Funes, and under the advise of local architect Alejandro Gonzalez, a friend of the owner, this little house by the sea gained much charm in its few meters. The masonry structure of the openings were all taken from recycled demolition purchased in Montevideo. The gabled roof, quite high, leaves enough room for a mezzanine bedroom and the bright interior provided by the ample windows and additional vents is fresh and clear. With whitewashed walls and floors, the simple decoration all point towards calm: big rest areas, supple bean bags, and a Paraguayan hammock, almost all white, combined with accessories in earth tones. True to the naturalist spirit of the oldest inhabitants and vacationers in the area, the ranch remains without electricity, dotted with lanterns and candles.

    Used throughout the year except in winter (the lack of stove would make the most enthusiastic cringe), the house is intended for two. At times, a couple of friends would come unexpectedly, but they always found them a corner to stay. They have to be rewarded for the effort, because to get to the house, which is somewhat removed from the village, they would have to leave the car on the route and take a 4x4 truck parked in the Cabo Polonio station in order to cross the dunes. From there, they would get on a horse-drawn carriage to the ranch. To return, the would have to arrange with for a driver who is commonly a sunbather encountered on the beach.

    Here, they do some cooking but they eat out a lot and spend most of the time on the beach. The few necessary supplies are bought in a store which is eight blocks of slow walking through the dunes. “You return with the best legs of your life, : no gym can give you the legs of Cabo Polonio" Gabriela makes fun and summing up her attachment to their small seaside retreat, she says: "Every time I have three days off, I’m running to there. "
